About Me

I am an undergraduate student in the School of Computing and Information Science at the University of Pittsburgh. I am majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Information Science, focused on software engineering, medical research, and cybersecurity. Throughout my time at Pitt, I have been involved with the Pitt Men's Glee Club, serving as Vice President, and also participated in lab research with the Surreality Lab under the UPMC Department of Neurosugery.

Projects

Tracer Awake

As a part of te Surreality Lab, I have served as technical lead, engineering a system to assist neurosurgeons during an awake craniotomy. With the purpose of analyzing differences in patients facial symmetry, as well as speech production, I have developed an app to run on an iPad. Written in Swift, it utilizes mediapipe to track the patients facial features, and a Wav2Vec model to break down the patients speech into comparable phonemes.

MicroSwir

Also under the Surreality Lab, I have assisted in Firmware developement for a shortwave infared camera used to distinguish types of second degree burns. Open-source firmware for the camera ran on a STM32 microprocessor, and was written in C. I had assisted in real-time data acquisition and sensor calibration to create a low-cost, portable SWIR platform for burn depth assessment and XR-guided surgical excision. .
